This Unit is about Structure

Organizational Structure is an important aspect that helps bring order to the complex procedures that are to be followed within an organization because it tells who does what. Although structure may not be the first aspect of an organization that is thought about, it must be given serious consideration, because it can directly impact the success of an organization. When designing an organizational structure, remember to take into account the available resources and goals of the organization you are working for.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • describe the basic dimensions of structure
  • describe traditional and emerging structural configurations
  • identify internal and external factors that impact structure design
  • identify potential advantages and disadvantages of common types of structure
  • discuss challenges of design in special situations (e.g. dot-com and global companies, mergers)
  • explain the importance of alignment between organizational structure and organizational strategy
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context for structure

Do:

  • compare and contrast differences among the basic or common structural configurations including potential advantages and disadvantages
  • identify how various factors impact structure design decisions
  • design an organization specifying organizational hierarchy, division of labor and coordinating mechanisms
  • design an organization for a given context
  • evaluate the alignment or fit between an organization’s structure and its strategic objectives for a given context

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • identify your preferred type of organizational structure
  • examine how your personal characteristics influence your preference for organizational culture type
  • evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of each organizational structure type
