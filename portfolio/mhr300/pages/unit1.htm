This Unit is about Strategy.

Strategy is an integral component to organizational effectiveness. It sets the standard for success, enables effective decision-making, and provides structure that facilitates day-to-day work and alignment of activities. Establishing a good strategic plan helps organizations to better understand who they are and their focus, particularly as we consider the overall market. Gaining a realistic view of the internal and external environment helps set an organization on the road to success.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • identify and describe benefits of strategic planning
  • describe concept and roles of strategic portfolio
  • describe basic stages of the strategic management process
  • describe major options for measuring strategic objectives
  • describe the role of operational plans
  • discuss tradeoffs between learning and planning

Do:

  • create a mission and vision statement for a specific organization
  • create strategic objectives for a specific organization and specify how to measure them
  • identify internal and external factors that impact a strategic plan in a given context
  • conduct a SWOT analysis
  • define an operational plan to achieve these strategic objectives

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • Decide if you’d like to take on the role of a Strategic Planner (or not).
  • Evaluate your ability to be an effective Strategic Planner.
  • Propose strategies to compensate for weaknesses in Strategic Planning ability.
  • Examine strategies for improving your Strategic Planning abilities.
  • Compare ‘best practices’ in developing or improving your Strategic Planning abilities
