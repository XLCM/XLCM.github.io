This Unit is about Teams

Some of our best experiences in work come through teams. Elite teams, like those we think of in examples like the Navy SEALS, can function an amazingly high levels and serve as important tools to address the challenges and opportunities organizations face. Teams can be good, bad, and ugly. Organizations must adapt their leadership styles, management techniques, human resource management strategies, and other functions to ensure that teams are an asset rather than a liability, maximizing their good, minimizing the bad, and eliminating the ugly.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • describe potential assets and liabilities of using teams in organizations
  • describe how to create an effective team
  • discuss the value and challenges of team diversity
  • identify the stages of team development
  • explain how to facilitate success at each stage of team development
  • explain what drives effective problem solving by individuals and teams
  • explain how to manage conflict within teams
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context for teams

Do:

  • create appropriate teams for a given context
  • develop a plan for managing teams taking into consideration the stage of development, diversity, and conflict potential
  • identify appropriate tools to manage team dynamics for a specific context
  • identify appropriate tools to manage team tasks for a given context

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • Identify your preferred Team Role and understand it’s key qualities
  • Evaluate the risks and rewards of managing by focusing on employee strengths

6.4 - Liabilities & Assets
14:30m

Teams are associated with a variety of assets and liabilities. The right collection of people into a group or team can yield a broader set of KSAOs, creating a broader set of perspectives on situations, issues, and events. Yet interactions can become more complicated, drawing out solutions, choosing compromise while diminishing solution quality, and/or exaggerate social pressures and tensions. When and how can teams overcome these potential liabilities to primarily bring assets to the organization?

6.5 - Team Building & ROI
10:40m

Organizations are concerned with understanding how their investment in teams can be measured and help yield success at the organizational level. Organizations must consider how teams can be effective and what constitutes over- or under-investing in them. A return on investment (ROI) analysis considers whether the cost of a team activity is worth it.
