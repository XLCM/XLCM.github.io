This Unit is about Change Management

Organizations survive and thrive through the generation and execution of good ideas. Unfortunately, the majority of good ideas fail to achieve their objectives because the change process is not well managed. You can increase the value you bring to an organization not only by generating good ideas, but also by developing skills to manage the change process.

This unit takes you through the essential elements of change management and then helps you build skills through a DOING assignment focused on how you would manage change based on information from a real organization.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • describe the change management process
  • describe how to assess an organization’s readiness for change
  • describe how to identify key stakeholders
  • discuss how stakeholders an facilitate or hinder change success
  • identify factors that impact stakeholder reaction to change
  • discuss techniques that can help obtain stakeholder support
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context for change management

Do:

  • analyze an organization’s readiness for change for a given context
  • identify key stakeholders for a given context
  • identify the stakeholder behaviors needed and expected for a given context
  • identify gaps between needed and expected stakeholder behaviors
  • create a change management plan to close anticipated stakeholder gaps and drive success
  • tell a “story” that persuasively communicates how change will facilitate strategic success

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • analyze your attitude towards organizational change

Team Stakeholder Analysis Project

Project Focus: Each team will conduct a stakeholder analysis related to an impending or potential change in an organizations identified by team members. The organizational change can be of any type as long as it is meaningful for the organization (e.g., an acquisition, a restructuring, creation of a new compensation system, introduction of a new software system, a change in leadership, a new product line).

This paper should include the following features:

  1. A description of the organization (or unit) in which the change will occur. This should include mission and vision statements for the organization (or unit) involved and the strategic objectives expected to be impacted by the change.

  2. A description of the change. Identify the nature of the change, the primary reasons for the change, and the measurable criteria against which the success of the change should be assessed. This section should be descriptive only.

  3. Identification of key stakeholders. Identify the key stakeholders and briefly explain why each identified stakeholder is a key stakeholder.

  4. Needed stakeholder behavior. Briefly describe the behavior needed from each key stakeholder to facilitate the success of the change and explain how this behavior would facilitate success of the change.

  5. Expected stakeholder behavior. Describe the behavior expected from each key stakeholder if not managed and the causes underlying this expected behavior.

  6. Potential gaps. Identify anticipated gaps between the needed stakeholder behavior identified in Part 4 and the expected behavior described in Part 5. Describe how the most significant of these gaps could hinder the success of the change.

  7. Plan to close one of the gaps. Identify at least one action for a significant gap identified in Part 6 that would help close the gap. Briefly explain why the recommended action should close the identified gap. In other words, tell a story that explains how/why your plan should work.

Parameters:

Write a comprehensive 2000 - 2400 word document that addresses each of the steps. Be clear, concise, and thorough in your response. Make sure to explain why you gave the answers you did. Make sure to include the web address for the organization you chose to evaluate.
