This Unit is about Entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship is a hot topic, particularly as we consider it within the context of the development of jobs and the creation of new businesses. Startup and small businesses account for the majority of US jobs and play a significant role in developing markets. Entrepreneurship comes in all shapes and sizes and goes far beyond the iconic examples, like Uber, Facebook, and Twitter, coming out of Silicon Valley. Today we have startups with a social mission, lifestyle companies, family-owned companies, crowdfunded companies, and many other types. This unit is aimed to examine the underlying principles behind effective entrepreneurial management.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • discuss the nature of the entrepreneurial process
  • discuss the nature of social entrepreneurship
  • describe how business models facilitate identification and exploitation of entrepreneurial opportunities
  • describe common business models for entrepreneurial ventures
  • identify the steps involved in starting a new venture
  • identify components of a business plan
  • identify the personal and organizational factors that drive entrepreneurial success
  • explain the role of innovation in entrepreneurship
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context for entrepreneurship and innovation

Do:

  • Develop a model for a high potential business venture
  • Identify the components of a basic plan for a new venture
  • develop a strategy to promote a new venture

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • assess your perception of your own adaptability
  • examine how your level of adaptability could impact your ability to manage uncertainty and change in organizations
  • develop strategies for identifying organizational cultures that match your adaptability style
  • develop strategies for adapting to organizational cultures that are at odds with your adaptability style

Start with taking the "Adaptability" Self-Assessment.

Interpret your results using this interpretation guide.

Then, consider the following:

Assume that you have recently graduated from college and have just been offered a job at Zappos. Zappos may be large but it is very much an entrepreneurial firm. You may recall, from earlier units in this course, that Zappos has a very unique organization culture and structure. Recently, Zappos CEO Tony Hsieh escalated the use of “holacracy” at Zappos, which is a very non-hierarchical management approach that emphasizes self-management rather than a traditional structure where everyone reports to a boss who in turn reports to a boss. Fourteen percent of the Zappos work force recently chose to leave after expressing displeasure with this organizational model. Many of the departures were attributed, at least in part, to preference for different organizational expectation.

Review your self-assessment results on adaptability. Given your results, would you be a good match or a poor match for a job at Zappos?

For your initial post:

If Zappos would be a good match for you, explain how / why your adaptability results would facilitate your success and happiness. Provide one specific suggestion for what you would look for in other organizations that would fit your adaptability style. If this would be a bad match, explain how / why your adaptability results would hinder your success and happiness at Zappos. Provide one specific suggestion for an action you could take to make yourself a better match for Zappos.

For your reply posts:

Find a post from a colleague who does think that Zappos would be a good fit for them. Do you agree with their suggestion for how to identify an organization that would be a good fit for them, based on their adaptability style? If so, explain why. If not, explain why and offer another suggestion for identifying organizations that would work for them.

Then, find a post from a colleague who does not think that Zappos would be a good fit for them. Do you agree with their suggestion for how to make themselves a better match for Zappos, based on their adaptability style? If so, explain why. If not, explain why and offer an alternate strategy for adapting themselves to an organization like Zappos.
