This Unit is about Leadership

Leadership is a topic that is often talked about in various contexts. What we know to be true about leadership is that there is more than one leadership type and that different leadership styles work best for certain organizations based on their mission. Having an effective leader in an organization is important because they can both positively and negatively influence the potential positive impacts of every other factor that has and will be discussed in this course.

Leadership styles need to be designed and executed in a way that is congruent with an organization’s strategy, structure, culture and human resource practices. The following lectures pose questions about the best leadership and organization matches needed to successfully accomplish organizational goals.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • describe how the personal characteristics, personality, and behaviors of leaders impact leadership success
  • explain the importance of interaction between organizational culture and leadership
  • explain the process for identifying appropriate leaders for a given context
  • explain why effective leadership behavior is context specific
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context
  • identify critical, ethical situations faced by leaders and discuss how organizational and individual values impact leader decisions

Do:

  • identify appropriate types of leader behaviors for a given context
  • identify actions that can enhance leader success in a given context
  • identify how followers can facilitate or hinder leader effectiveness and efficiency
  • identify qualitative and quantitative methods to evaluate leader success
  • design a program to train and develop leaders in a given context

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • decide if you’d like to take on a leadership role in your future career (or not)
  • identify your preferred leadership style
  • examine positive and negative leadership qualities in others
  • formulate strategies to improve your leadership abilities
  • identify universal qualities of good leaders

4.8 - Leadership Theory Implications
9:00m

The different leadership styles previously discussed all have specific characteristics that distinguish them. However, for all of the models, the leadership goals are for followers to contribute to organizational goals, followers to achieve personal goals, followers to become engaged in the organization and committed to the success of the organization, and followers to be satisfied with the leadership process.

4.9 - Special Considerations
11:05m

This last lecture addresses leader neutralizers and substitutes. Neutralizers can reduce the effect of the efforts of becoming effective leaders. On the other hand, substitutes can be used systematically to enhance the effectiveness of a leader. Neutralizers and substitutes are factors that can either diminish or enhance the work of a leader.
