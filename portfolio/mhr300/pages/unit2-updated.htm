This Unit is about Culture.

The values, norms, and beliefs shared by an organization’s members can help the organization gain competitive advantage by developing and managing a strong, healthy culture. Each organization may choose a different type of culture. It is important to identify the primary functions of that culture, its major elements or characteristics, and how it can best be developed, shared, transmitted, maintained, and even changed to evolve with an organization, rather than against an organization.

At the end of this Unit, you should be able to:

Know:

  • compare and contrast organizational and national cultures
  • describe the basic elements of culture
  • describe how culture develops
  • describe how culture is transmitted, maintained and changed
  • describe how culture can create a competitive advantage for the organization
  • describe how culture can hinder organizational success
  • describe how organizational culture can be assessed
  • describe the importance of fit between organizational culture and employee values
  • identify and discuss the implications of a global context for organizational culture

Do:

  • identify elements of culture for a given context
  • evaluate the extent to which culture supports the strategic objectives of the organization for a given context
  • identify appropriate actions to transmit and maintain culture in the organization
  • identify appropriate actions to change culture in the organization
  • evaluate the fit between organizational culture and employee values for a given context

Be / Inspire / Network:

  • Identify your preferred type of organizational culture
  • Evaluate your ‘goodness of fit’ with different organizational culture types
  • Examine the pros and cons of each organizational culture type
  • Describe strategies to identify a company's organizational culture
  • Investigate the potential risks of working in an organization that is at odds with your culture preference
