Course Design @ The University of Washington - Foster School of Business

I served as the lead instructional designer for both of these courses, which represent the typical style of our projects.

  • Successful Negotiations

    Elizabeth was initially concerned about how her traditional face-to-face course would work online. The core activity of her course, which involved face-to-face mock negotiations between students, would be lost.

    Through the use of Zoom to record these sessions, students were able to review their negotiation performance and do deep reflective analysis of their performance afterwards. The instructor was able to review these recorded sessions and respond to student comments on their performance, and offer her own critique.

    This course culminated in a complex, multigroup negotiation session with multiple stakeholders conducted via Zoom.

  • Product Management

    Jeff was able to augment his course with synchronous online collaboration tools like Slack and Trello to conduct collaborative brainstorming sessions that he could observe and participate in. Zoom allowed students to record product pitches, and share those recordings with other students for critique.

Course Build Automation

After developing the style guide for all of the courses in our program, I wanted to automate the process of converting course plans to Canvas LMS content. Since we were using Google Docs, I was able to develop a backend Javascript app that automated this process.

This script treats the document like a table, iterates through the table's rows and cells, matches the cell content with a set of keywords, and wraps the cell's content in the appropriate HTML tags.

  • This Module Blueprint document is where faculty would develop and document their content.
  • This Google Apps project contains all of the programming logic.
  • And this container document receives the app output, which is copied into our LMS.

Course Design @ UW-Madison

I was excited to be a part of UW Madison's first foray into the MOOC space with a series of MOOCs we build between 2013-2016. I was the lead instructional designer for two MOOCs, listed below. These courses were seen by ~29,000 students around the world.

The instructional design model here was unique; something I'd describe more as 'edutainment.' They were intended to give casual students entertaining and informative content without the commitment of deliverables or a timetable, and more serious students had the opportunity to participate in discussions and participate in hands-on activities.
